🫃 Digestive System

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients that the body can use for energy, growth and repair.

OrganWhat it does
MouthChews food and mixes it with saliva to begin digestion.
EsophagusTube that carries food from the mouth to the stomach.
StomachMixes food with acids and digestive juices. Breaks it into a mushy liquid.
Small intestineAbsorbs nutrients from food into the bloodstream. Most digestion happens here.
Large intestineAbsorbs water. Turns leftover material into solid waste.
Rectum & AnusStore and remove solid waste from the body.
The journey of food:
Mouth → Esophagus → Stomach → Small intestine → Large intestine → Out
Digestion starts in the MOUTH, not the stomach! Saliva already begins breaking down food as you chew.

🫀 How Body Systems Work Together

The body systems do not work alone — they all depend on each other to keep the body healthy and functioning.

SystemMain jobWorks with
DigestiveBreaks down food into nutrientsCirculatory (delivers nutrients)
RespiratoryBrings oxygen in, removes CO2Circulatory (carries oxygen)
CirculatoryTransports blood, oxygen & nutrientsAll systems
Example of teamwork:
1. You eat food → Digestive system breaks it into nutrients
2. Nutrients enter the blood → Circulatory system carries them
3. You breathe in → Respiratory system adds oxygen to blood
4. Blood delivers oxygen + nutrients to all cells
All systems share one goal: keep every cell in the body alive and working!

🥗 Nutrition and Healthy Eating

What we eat directly affects how our body works. A balanced diet gives the body everything it needs.

🍞CarbohydratesMain source of energy
Bread, rice, pasta, potato
🥩ProteinsBuild and repair muscles and cells
Meat, eggs, beans, milk
🥑FatsEnergy reserve and organ protection
Olive oil, avocado, nuts
🍊VitaminsRegulate body functions and immunity
Fruits, vegetables, greens
🥛Mineral SaltsStrengthen bones, teeth and muscles
Calcium (milk), Iron (meat)
💧WaterEssential for all body functions
Drink 6–8 glasses per day
How eating habits affect the body:
Good habits (balanced diet, exercise, water, sleep) → body systems work in harmony
Poor habits (too much sugar/fat, no exercise) → can lead to obesity, heart disease, low energy
No single food has everything! That is why we need to eat a VARIETY of foods from different groups every day.
